# GoingBulk Project Overview

## Working Identity

GoingBulk is a public, data-driven fitness and health brand built around a real personal transformation.

The core idea is simple:

```text
Fitness transformation with the data left on.
```

GoingBulk will document nutrition, training, supplements, body metrics, bloodwork, DEXA scans, wearable data, product usage, and structured personal experiments over time.

## Positioning

GoingBulk should not be positioned as another generic fitness influencer brand.

The stronger position is:

```text
A public personal fitness dataset, experiment log, and product-testing media brand.
```

The audience watches the transformation, but the differentiator is the public evidence trail.

## Core Promise

GoingBulk publishes the receipts.

That means:

- food and macros are logged;
- training is logged;
- supplements are tracked by dose, date, and adherence;
- body metrics are tracked over time;
- DEXA, bloodwork, and device data are compared when available;
- experiments define baseline, intervention, result, confounders, and limitations;
- product reviews disclose compensation and separate claims from personal results.

## Main Audiences

### General Audience

People who want a clear, understandable view of the transformation.

They need:

- simple charts;
- weekly progress summaries;
- food and workout examples;
- before/during/after views;
- plain-language experiment verdicts;
- practical cost, adherence, and product takeaways.

### Professionals

Doctors, registered dietitians, nutritionists, exercise scientists, coaches, and serious data-minded viewers.

They need:

- methodology;
- date ranges;
- source labels;
- confidence levels;
- confounders;
- limitations;
- exports or structured summaries;
- clear separation between research, claim, protocol, and personal result.

### Sponsors and Product Partners

Brands that may want product reviews, affiliate placement, or tracked product tests.

They need:

- transparent review pages;
- disclosure policy;
- product-use windows;
- before/during/after context;
- data confidence labels;
- clear editorial independence.

## Brand Style

GoingBulk should be:

- curious;
- transparent;
- skeptical but fair;
- data-first;
- human and entertaining;
- not a drama channel;
- not a medical authority;
- honest about uncertainty.

## Core Content Format

The signature format is:

```text
Claim or question
-> research review
-> personal protocol
-> tracked experiment
-> data summary
-> limitations
-> personal verdict
```

This allows GoingBulk to test study-inspired fitness, nutrition, supplement, and health claims without pretending one person's result proves a universal outcome.

## Key Differentiator

Most fitness content says what someone did.

GoingBulk should show:

- what was planned;
- what actually happened;
- what data changed;
- what did not change;
- what confounders existed;
- what conclusion is reasonable;
- what conclusion would be overclaiming.

## First Major Public Mission

A strong first public mission would be:

```text
Build a 12-week baseline-to-bulk dataset using Cronometer imports, workout logs, body metrics, Hume/consumer device data, DEXA scans, bloodwork, and progress photos.
```

This creates a clean origin story, early dashboard material, and a foundation for later product tests and experiments.

## Non-Goals

GoingBulk is not:

- a clinical trial;
- medical advice;
- a supplement hype channel;
- a takedown or drama channel;
- a replacement for Cronometer;
- a replacement for doctors, dietitians, or coaches;
- a claim that N=1 data proves broad causation.

## Guiding Principle

```text
Data first. Disclosure always. Money never edits the conclusion.
```
