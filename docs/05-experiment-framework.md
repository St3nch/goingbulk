# GoingBulk Experiment Framework

## Purpose

Experiments are the core content and data unit of GoingBulk.

GoingBulk should not be built around random blog posts. It should be built around structured experiments, public logs, and honest personal results.

## Signature Format

```text
Claim or question
-> research/source review
-> personal protocol
-> baseline
-> intervention
-> follow-up when useful
-> data summary
-> confounders
-> limitations
-> personal verdict
```

## Experiment Types

### Study-Inspired Experiments

These are based on a research paper, systematic review, or public health/nutrition claim grounded in research.

Example:

```text
A study reports that a specific dietary habit may affect blood pressure.
GoingBulk reviews the study, defines a safe personal protocol, tracks baseline data, tests it, and reports personal results.
```

### Product or Supplement Experiments

These track a product, supplement, device, app, or service over a defined window.

Examples:

- creatine protocol;
- Hume Pod vs DEXA comparison;
- protein powder review;
- wearable recovery tracking;
- meal service test.

### Training Experiments

These test workout programming, training volume, recovery, or progression approaches.

Examples:

- 12-week hypertrophy block;
- push/pull/legs program;
- increased weekly leg volume;
- deload strategy.

### Lifestyle Experiments

These test habits or behavior changes.

Examples:

- walking after meals;
- sleep schedule adjustment;
- caffeine cutoff time;
- hydration target;
- step goal.

## Experiment Page Structure

Each experiment should have a canonical page.

Suggested sections:

1. Summary
2. Claim or question
3. Source/research review
4. Hypothesis
5. Protocol
6. Baseline data
7. Intervention data
8. Follow-up data, if applicable
9. Results
10. Confounders
11. Limitations
12. Verdict
13. Would I keep doing it?
14. Related products/supplements
15. Related content/social posts
16. Data source and confidence notes

## N=1 Posture

GoingBulk experiments are personal N=1 documentation unless explicitly stated otherwise.

Standard disclaimer:

```text
This GoingBulk experiment is an N=1 personal tracking project. It is not a clinical trial, medical advice, or a replication of the original study. The goal is to document what happened to me under the protocol described here.
```

## Research Review Template

For study-inspired experiments, include:

```text
What was studied?
Who was studied?
How many participants?
How long did it run?
What dose/protocol was used?
What was the control/comparison?
What outcomes changed?
How large was the effect?
What limitations existed?
How similar or different am I from the studied population?
```

## Claim Separation Rule

Every experiment should separate:

```text
Influencer/public claim
Research finding
GoingBulk personal protocol
GoingBulk personal result
Reasonable verdict
```

Do not collapse those into one claim.

## Verdict Labels

Suggested verdicts:

- Worked for me
- Maybe helped
- No clear change
- Inconclusive
- Not worth repeating
- Worth retesting
- Do not recommend

Avoid universal verdicts such as:

- Proven
- Debunked
- Guaranteed
- Cures
- Fixes

## Confidence Rating

Each experiment should have a confidence rating.

Suggested scale:

```text
Low: too short, noisy, many confounders, or weak measurement quality.
Medium: useful signal but still meaningful limitations.
High: longer duration, good adherence, stable context, strong measurement quality.
```

High confidence in an N=1 experiment still does not prove a universal claim.

## Confounder Log

Track anything that could distort results:

- sleep changes;
- stress;
- illness;
- injury;
- medication changes;
- calorie changes;
- protein changes;
- sodium changes;
- caffeine changes;
- alcohol;
- travel;
- missed workouts;
- missed supplement doses;
- training program changes;
- hydration changes.

## Baseline, Intervention, Follow-Up

Minimum useful structure:

```text
Baseline: 7-14 days
Intervention: 14-60+ days depending on claim/product
Follow-up: 7-14 days when stopping or washout is relevant
```

For major experiments, longer windows are better.

## Claim Queue

GoingBulk should maintain a backlog of potential experiments.

Fields:

```text
claim_text
source_type
source_url
research_url
topic
intervention
target_metric
risk_level
evidence_quality
testability
content_potential
status
```

Statuses:

```text
captured
researching
rejected
approved_to_test
baseline
active
completed
published
```

## Safety Gate

Some claims should not be tested personally.

Do not test:

- stopping or changing prescribed medication;
- high-dose supplements without professional guidance;
- dangerous stimulant stacks;
- extreme dehydration;
- extreme fasting;
- research chemicals;
- anything likely to create meaningful health risk for content.

A valid experiment outcome can be:

```text
Researched and rejected because the risk/reward did not make sense.
```

## Drama Avoidance

GoingBulk tests claims, not people.

Preferred framing:

```text
I saw this claim, reviewed the source, and tested a practical version on myself.
```

Avoid:

```text
This creator lied.
This video is exposed.
This proves everyone wrong.
```

## Content Flywheel

One experiment can create:

- YouTube long-form video;
- shorts/reels/TikTok updates;
- X thread;
- newsletter update;
- website experiment page;
- data dashboard;
- product review context;
- professional-facing report.

## Core Rule

```text
Experiments are the backbone. Blog posts support experiments, not the other way around.
```
