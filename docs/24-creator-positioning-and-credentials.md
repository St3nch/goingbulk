# GoingBulk Creator Positioning and Credentials

## Purpose

This document defines the human positioning behind GoingBulk.

GoingBulk cannot only be a data platform. The public needs to understand who is behind the data, why the project exists, what credentials the creator has or does not have, and why the audience should care about this particular N=1 fitness and health dataset.

## Core Problem This Solves

A public dataset from an unknown person is not automatically compelling.

GoingBulk needs a clear answer to:

```text
Why should anyone care about this person's fitness and health data?
```

## Working Positioning

GoingBulk should be positioned as:

```text
A transparent personal fitness transformation and experimentation project where the creator publicly tracks nutrition, training, supplements, body metrics, devices, labs, products, and limitations with the receipts left on.
```

The creator is not positioned as a medical authority unless actual credentials justify that.

The strongest default posture is:

```text
I am not asking you to trust me because I am an authority.
I am asking you to inspect the process, the data, the methods, and the limitations.
```

## Creator Identity Decision

The creator must decide whether GoingBulk uses a real name, public persona, or pseudonym.

### Option A: Real Name

Pros:

- stronger trust;
- better E-E-A-T/person schema;
- easier expert outreach;
- better sponsor credibility;
- easier YouTube/social brand building.

Cons:

- privacy risk;
- health data permanently tied to real identity;
- higher doxxing/harassment risk;
- progress photos and lab data become harder to separate from personal life.

### Option B: Public Persona / Brand Name

Pros:

- some privacy protection;
- still human and memorable;
- can reveal more later;
- allows controlled identity boundary.

Cons:

- less authority than real name;
- may reduce sponsor/expert trust;
- harder to build personal credibility.

### Option C: Fully Pseudonymous

Pros:

- strongest privacy protection;
- easier to publish sensitive data;
- lowers personal exposure.

Cons:

- weaker trust;
- harder to attract doctors/nutritionists;
- weaker sponsor credibility;
- harder to build YouTube/social influence.

## Recommended Default

For GoingBulk, the likely best default is:

```text
Public persona with controlled identity disclosure.
```

This allows the project to feel human while still preserving some privacy.

This can evolve into real-name disclosure later if the creator chooses.

## Credentials Statement

GoingBulk should explicitly state credentials or lack of credentials.

### If No Formal Health Credentials

Use language like:

```text
I am not a doctor, registered dietitian, nutritionist, or licensed healthcare professional. GoingBulk is a personal documentation project. I publish my methods, data sources, limitations, and confounders so others can inspect the process instead of relying on authority claims.
```

### If Some Relevant Credentials Exist

Use language like:

```text
I have [credential/background], but GoingBulk experiments are still N=1 personal documentation and should not be treated as medical advice, clinical evidence, or a recommendation for others to copy.
```

### If Fitness/Technical Background Only

Use language like:

```text
My background is in [technical/fitness/personal area], not medicine. GoingBulk combines personal discipline, structured logging, and transparent reporting. Professional interpretation is welcomed, especially where health markers or risk-sensitive topics are involved.
```

## Trust Positioning

GoingBulk should build trust through:

- transparent methods;
- public limitations;
- source/confidence labels;
- visible confounders;
- clear disclaimers;
- data exports;
- no hidden sponsor influence;
- willingness to publish boring or negative results;
- honest correction history;
- expert critique when available.

Trust should not rely on pretending the creator is more qualified than they are.

## Story Angles

Potential story frameworks:

### Transformation Story

```text
I am documenting a real body/fitness transformation with public data, not just before-and-after photos.
```

### Data Transparency Story

```text
Most fitness content shows conclusions. GoingBulk shows the process, receipts, and limitations.
```

### Claim Testing Story

```text
Instead of arguing about health and fitness claims, I review the evidence, test safe personal versions, and publish what happened to me.
```

### Product Testing Story

```text
If I use a product, supplement, device, or service, I track the usage window and disclose whether money was involved.
```

## About Page Requirements

The public About page should answer:

1. Who is behind GoingBulk?
2. Why did GoingBulk start?
3. What is being tracked?
4. What does N=1 mean?
5. What credentials does the creator have or not have?
6. What should readers not assume?
7. How does GoingBulk handle sponsors and affiliates?
8. How can experts critique the project?

## Person Schema Considerations

If the creator uses a real name or stable public persona, the site can use Person schema.

Person schema should only include accurate public information.

Avoid adding credentials, affiliations, or expertise claims that are not true.

## Expert Outreach Positioning

GoingBulk should invite critique without pretending endorsement.

Example:

```text
I welcome critique from doctors, registered dietitians, nutritionists, exercise scientists, and coaches. Expert comments may be referenced with permission, but GoingBulk does not imply professional endorsement unless explicitly stated.
```

## Sponsor Positioning

Sponsors should understand:

```text
GoingBulk is transparent first and commercial second.
```

The creator's public identity and credibility depend on honest conclusions.

## Hard Boundaries

GoingBulk must not claim:

- that the creator is a medical professional unless true;
- that personal results apply to everyone;
- that the project provides treatment guidance;
- that experiments prove causation;
- that sponsors do not influence content unless the contract actually preserves editorial independence.

## Open Questions For The Creator

Answer before public launch:

1. Will GoingBulk use a real name, public persona, or pseudonym?
2. Will the creator show face on the website and YouTube?
3. What health/fitness starting point is safe to share?
4. What data is permanently off-limits?
5. What credentials or lack of credentials will be stated?
6. What is the main transformation goal?
7. What is the first 90-day public mission?
8. How much vulnerability is acceptable?
9. What expert critique is wanted?
10. What sponsor categories are off-limits?

## Core Rule

```text
GoingBulk credibility comes from transparent process, not borrowed authority.
```
